Model Overview

What this model does

This project is a financial forecasting system powered by a deep neural network. Its purpose is to estimate how a company’s financial statement may evolve over time by looking at both the company’s historical financial data and the industry conditions surrounding it.

Starting from a selected base year, the model uses key financial values from the company’s uploaded statement and combines them with industry level indicators such as revenue change, employment change, and wage change. It then predicts the next year’s financial values. For longer forecasts, each newly predicted year is fed back into the model to generate the following year, allowing the system to produce a multi year projection rather than a single one time estimate.

In practical terms, the model acts like a forecasting engine. It does not simply label a company as good or bad. Instead, it produces projected numerical financial outputs that can help users understand where a company’s revenue, profit, and expenses may be heading under current conditions.

Forecast Pipeline

How the system works

A company file is parsed, industry trends are added, and the model forecasts the next financial year before repeating the process for future years.

01

Upload financial statements

The user uploads an Excel file containing company financial statement data.

02

Extract company metrics

The system pulls revenue, gross profit, interest expense, operating expense, tax, and net income from the uploaded file.

03

Add industry context

Using the company’s SIC and NAICS mapping, the app adds revenue, employment, and wage trend data from industry report files.

04

Generate forecasts

A deep neural network predicts the next year’s financial values, then repeats that process recursively for multi year forecasting.

Why this approach matters

More than a simple spreadsheet projection

Traditional forecasting methods often rely only on a company’s past values. This system goes further by incorporating industry conditions as well. That means the forecast is shaped not only by what the company did previously, but also by the wider business environment represented through industry trend indicators.

By combining these two sources of information in a deep learning pipeline, the project aims to create a more informed simulation of how a company’s finances may develop over time.
